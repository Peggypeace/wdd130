White Water Rafting

By the 1970s, advances in inflatable raft design made rafting safer and more accessible. This period saw the rise of dedicated rafting companies, many founded by former river guides who wanted to share the thrill of whitewater with tourists. Organizations like OARS (founded in 1969) became pioneers, offering multi-day rafting adventures in the American West.

History

Whitewater rafting as a commercial activity began in the mid-20th century. While rafting itself had existed earlier for exploration and transport, it wasn’t until the 1950s and 1960s that outfitters started offering guided trips for recreation. Early commercial rafting was popularized on rivers like the Snake River in Wyoming, the Colorado River through the Grand Canyon, and rivers in Idaho.
